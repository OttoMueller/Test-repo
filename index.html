About Me

My name is Thomas Mueller and I am an 18 year old student attending the College of Wooster. I have a twin who is at Saint Lawerence and an older brother who is also at the College of Wooster. Currently, my major is undeciced but I have taken an interest in Political Science, preferably something along the lines with International Relations. I have been interested with politics for a while now but my interest only grew after the 2016 election.

During my first semester at C.O.W, I have completed two political science and two history courses. All of these courses interested me greatly due to how much I was able to take away from them (and the fact that I like history and politics). In my second semester, I chose to take two history classes, a digital humanitites class, and an economics class. Though I am not taking a Politcial Science course, I am wanting to minor in History, which I have officially decided to do. Outside of classes, I am a member of the Mens Ultimate Frisbee team. Unfortunatly, I have been struggling when it comes to memorizing the plays.

Outside of school, I enjoy walking my dog, reading books, and building with legos. I love exercising my mind when it comes to creating something new, which is the appeal I get from legos. Ironically, I do not want to go into engineering even though that would seem right up my ally. I enjoy small designs, not large designs. Apart from legos, I am passionate about Star Wars and other stories that offer a type of "escapism" that was popular back when cities were growing. However, instead of imagining an era involving the unknown wilderness and the Revolutionary War, my imagination goes to an era with either advanced technology or an era with mystical forces; whether it be in a modern or Medieval enviorment.

Work

When I was in my Sophmore year of high school, I worked at Kopps for two years, making french fries, onion rings, occasionally baggiong and even cooking burgers. I mostly worked as "clean up", which is basically keeping the bath rooms clean, the "foyer" clean, dishes washed, and taking out the trash. At the end of my Senior year of High School, I began working at a restraunt called "The Chocolate Factory". While there, I worked as a "scooper" who basically scooped ice cream for customers. "Scoopers" would also work the cash register, which was surprisingly stressful at times. Despite the stress, I was able to improve my people skills while woprking there, learning how to work with cuatomers who were either oblivious to what they wanted or were straight up obnoxious.

Outside of school, I have worked with the Appalachian Service Project twice, an organization that goes to different parts of the United States and spends a week working on impoverished houses. I have also worked with Christ Church, helping organize church related events for the entire congregation. These events include block parties as well as social gatherings. I have participated with several fundraisers which included washing cars to raise money for Christ Church as well as packing and gathering food for the Hunger Task Force (both through and out of church).
